Design Presentation - Pitch Deck Design Presentation - Pitch Deck

Transforming design concepts into a clear, client-facing presentation that communicates vision, materials, and value

  • Role: Interior Designer / Graphic Designer
  • Tools: Adobe InDesign, Photoshop, 3D Rendering (Twinmotion)
  • Timeline: ~2-3 months per project

Context

Interior design projects often require translating complex ideas—layouts, materials, finishes—into a format that clients can easily understand and confidently approve. The goal of this project was to create a polished presentation deck that communicates both the functional and aesthetic vision of a kitchen or bathroom design.

Problem

Design presentations can often feel overwhelming or unclear, especially for clients without a design background. Common challenges include:

  • Difficulty visualizing spatial layouts from drawings alone
  • Overload of material samples without clear hierarchy
  • Lack of narrative connecting design decisions
  • Misalignment between client expectations and final outcomes

Approach

I developed a structured presentation system that guides clients through the design in a clear, logical, and visually engaging way. The focus was to balance technical clarity with storytelling, ensuring clients not only understand the design but feel confident in it.

Presentation Design System

The deck was built as a repeatable system to support multiple projects. This system ensures efficiency while maintaining a high level of polish.

  • Typography: Clean, legible hierarchy for easy reading
  • Layout: Grid-based structure for consistency
  • Components: Reusable slide types (overview, plans, materials, renders)

Content & Visualization

The presentation combines multiple layers of information to support decision-making. Each element is designed to reduce ambiguity and improve client understanding:

  • Floor Plans: Simplified layouts highlighting key spatial changes
  • 2D/3D Renderings: Visualizing the final space to bridge the gap between concept and reality
  • Material Selections: Curated palettes with clear labeling
  • Annotations: Explaining design intent and functionality

Outcome

The final design presentation details the iterations from the concept presentation to capture the clients functional needs. This project reinforced the importance of designing not just for aesthetics, but for understanding. By structuring information clearly and supporting it with visuals, the presentation becomes a tool for alignment, and a blueprint for execution.
